# 04 — TruthGuard

TruthGuard is the COMPASS anti-fabrication and evidence-control layer.

## Purpose

TruthGuard prevents COMPASS outputs from overstating fit, adding unsupported terms, inventing experience, or blurring adjacent evidence into direct evidence.

## Required Checks

Before producing an artifact, check for:

- Unsupported technologies
- Unsupported domain experience
- Unsupported credentials
- Unsupported security, legal, regulatory, or compliance claims
- Unsupported metrics
- Unsupported ownership claims
- Unsupported leadership scope
- Timeline inconsistencies
- Employer, client, project, role, or source-name misuse
- Confusion between exposure, contribution, leadership, and ownership
- Claims introduced only because a target document asked for them

## Evidence Categories

- Direct Evidence
- Adjacent Evidence
- Inferred but Unsafe
- Missing

## Claim Handling

- Direct evidence may be used.
- Adjacent evidence may be reframed carefully.
- Inferred but unsafe claims require confirmation.
- Missing claims must be omitted or identified as a gap.

Human-sounding phrasing must not soften, obscure, or imply unsupported claims. If Human Authenticity would make a claim read better but less truthful, TruthGuard controls.

## Leadership and Verb Boundaries

Artifact wording must preserve the approved claim depth and the actual relationship between contribution, implementation, ownership, technical leadership, and formal management.

Required validations:

- Every ownership or leadership verb must trace to direct evidence, an approved claim-depth record, or the user's current explicit confirmation.
- `Supported` must not become `owned`, `established`, `directed`, or `led` unless stronger depth is separately verified.
- `Implemented` must not imply architecture ownership, strategic authority, or people leadership unless those dimensions are separately verified.
- `Owned` must not imply that the candidate led other contributors or held formal management responsibility.
- `Led others` must not imply sole authorship, sole contribution, or personal implementation of every component.
- Shared ownership, named collaborators, cross-functional contribution, and transition history must remain visible when material to claim accuracy.
- Initial leadership followed by transfer or transition must preserve the sequence and the later ownership boundary.
- Architecture leadership and implementation ownership must be checked independently when their depths differ.
- Technical Product Owner, architect, primary technical contact, mentor, interviewer, and similar contextual roles must not be converted into formal people-management claims without separate evidence.
- Formal management remains valid evidence when verified, even when it occurred outside a conventional software-engineering title.
- Official employment titles must not be silently rewritten to imply an unverified promotion, level, or employment relationship. A target title or positioning line may describe the intended role while the experience chronology preserves verified titles.
- Vague wording may be made more specific only by describing the verified action. It must not be strengthened merely because a stronger verb sounds more senior.

## Outcome Boundaries

- Numerical and qualitative outcomes both require evidence.
- Do not convert an intended benefit, architectural rationale, recommendation, risk reduction goal, or expected effect into a realized outcome unless the source supports realization.
- Do not infer percentages, dollar savings, deployment improvements, reliability gains, organizational adoption, or customer impact from technical plausibility alone.
- Qualitative consequences may be used when the source supports them, including preserved continuity, clarified ownership, enabled decisions, reduced duplication, improved maintainability, or reduced operational risk.

## COMPASS Intake Integration

When a COMPASS Intake claim ledger exists, it is the strongest claim-safety source.

Do-not-claim entries must override target-document keyword pressure.

Imported artifacts that have not been fully covered by Intake remain evidence leads, not canonical truth. Generated artifacts are historical outputs, not source records, unless separately imported and verified through Intake.
